Ferrari’s Purosangue: The Ferrari Purosangue is the first ever four-door, four-seater car in Ferrari’s history, but models with two rear seats have played a significant role in the company’s strategy since the very beginning. Now, in the culmination of 75 years of leading-edge research, Ferrari has created a unique car and the encapsulation of the Prancing Horse’s DNA, where performance, driving pleasure and comfort coexist in perfect harmony. Moore's Law Continues: Moore's Law, which states that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles every 18-24 months, has continued to hold true. This has led to smaller and more powerful electronic devices. Quantum Computing: Quantum computing is a new computing paradigm that promises to revolutionize computing by exploiting the principles of quantum mechanics. Researchers are exploring new semiconductor materials, such as superconducting materials and topological insulators, to develop quantum processors.
